Some of the best moments I've had in my life have come snowboarding on a day with crisp, white powder.
And although this isn't me in this picture, I still have a lot of fun playing around with how the board glides through the snow and turns with my movements.


But enough about me - here's a short story about the real icon of this page, Shaun White!

In 2009, Shaun White was training for the Winter X Games when he decided to push the limits of snowboarding with a trick that had never been done before. He spent months working on the Double McTwist 1260, a trick involving two flips and three-and-a-half spins. White took a hard fall during practice, breaking a helmet and getting a black eye, but he didn't give up. His perseverance paid off at the Winter X Games when he landed the trick perfectly, leaving the audience and judges in awe. This groundbreaking move not only earned him the gold medal but also redefined the possibilities in the sport of snowboarding.

Timeline of a few of White's achievements:
  1. 2006 Winter Olympics in Italy
    • Men's halfpipe
    • Shaun brought home the gold with a 46.8 score
  1. 2010 Winter Olympics in Canada
    • Men's halfpipe

  1. 2018 Winter Olympics in South Korea
    • Men's halfpipe
    • Guess who brought home the gold again ? this time with a 98.5 score
